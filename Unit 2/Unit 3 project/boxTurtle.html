Box Turtles

Box turtles are turtles of the genus Terrapene. Although box turtles are very similar to tortoises in terrestrial habits and in appearance, they are members of the American pond turtle family.

Nine diffferent box tutle specices are apart of the list of many animals on the endangered specices list. The species of box turtle on the list are the Pan's box turtle, McCord's box turtle, and the Chinese three-striped box turtle, which live in China, and the Bourret's box turtle, which is native to Vietnam and Laos. Sadly the population estimates for these species are unknown at this time. The Yunnan box turtle, also native to China, may already be extinct in the wild, with estimates of only 0-50 individual turtles remaining. The southern Vietnam box turtle, Zhou's box turtle, the Indochinese box turtle, and the yellow-headed box turtle are also the endangered.
