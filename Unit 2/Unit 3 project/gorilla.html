Gorilla

They are reportedly the largest of the great apes, and are stocky animals with broad chests and shoulders, large, human-like hands, and small eyes set into hairless faces. The two gorilla species live in equatorial Africa, separated by about 560 miles of Congo Basin forest. Each has a lowland and upland subspecies.

There are two species of gorillas, who of which are endangered the eastern gorilla and western gorilla. There are estimated to only be 2,600 eastern gorillas remaining in East Africa, and while the number of western gorillas is unknown at the moment, but their population is reported to be decreasing. Mountain gorillas, a subspecies of the eastern gorilla, are said to only have about 600 remaining. However, their population is reportedly increasing.
